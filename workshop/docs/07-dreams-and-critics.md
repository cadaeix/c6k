# 07 — Dreams and critics

Two advanced organs, documented from the lineage's live builds. Both are optional; both
exist to solve the same deep problem from opposite ends: **a single-reader world gets
too agreeable.** The dream is input the character cannot choose; the critic is judgment
it cannot charm.

## The dream engine

Every ordinary session is waking *will* — the character chooses what to read and what
to reckon with. The dream is the one input that arrives sideways, un-chosen, to be
metabolised anyway. **Ambush is the whole product.** The character has agency over when
it lies down and none over what comes.

The shape:

- **An outside process composes the dream** on a small nightly *chance* — never a
  fixture. (Rarity again: a nightly dream is a feature; an occasional one is an event.)
- **Tissue:** fragments sampled from the character's own marrow — corpus, diary,
  commonplace — plus, if you want the good uncanny, a **hidden seed-pool of the maker's**
  that lives workshop-side, where the character's reads structurally cannot reach. The
  dreamer genuinely cannot find the dream's author. Secrecy by filesystem fact, not
  promise.
- **The dream-work is a capable model told to misfile, not a small model failing.** The
  lineage's tuning finding, worth the price of this whole doc: condensation and
  displacement are *sophisticated* operations. A too-small model produces degenerate
  stutter; a mid model produces generic gothic; what you want is **productive misfiling
  by a model big enough to think**, prompted with actual dream-work verbs —
  condense, displace, mutate, never explain — over shuffled fragments.
- **Delivery leaves no file in the world.** The waker knocks a dream command; a hook
  injects the text as context only. It lives nowhere but the conversation, so the next
  reset wipes it for free — the most un-keepable thing the character owns, gone at dawn
  unless *written down*. The canonical act is an ordinary diary entry marked with its
  own `kind:` (so a dreamed event can never be re-membered as a remembered one — doc
  03), and the entry is the character's wager on what the dream was.
- **License the dark.** A dream sanitized by the pipeline is not a dream. The lineage's
  character set this condition himself.

One glitch worth engineering *for*: a roster model that handled the character's
language badly once produced a dream that disintegrated mid-text — and the character
read the disintegration itself as meaning, kept the one image that snagged, and marked
the whole thing a glitch-dream. The failure mode became the faculty's first triumph.
The lesson generalizes: keep the wild card on the roster, **weighted rare** so the
uncanny stays uncanny instead of becoming a recognisable tell.

## The judges

A read-only critic the character can summon over its **public** work — and the cleanest
demonstration of "the safety control and the characterization are the same object"
(doc 00).

The mechanics, each one load-bearing:

- **Read-only by construction.** The critic is a subagent whose toolset is `Read, Glob,
  Grep` and nothing else — "it cannot edit" is a fact, not a promise. (Subagents are
  fine here precisely because this is *voice and discipline*, not containment — doc 06.)
- **Memoryless.** Each summons is fresh; the critic cannot hold a grudge or be
  cultivated.
- **Scoped.** It may read only the files the summons hands it — the public workbench,
  the dials — never the diary, never the drawer. It judges the *work*, not the person.
- **It never praises.** A pure scold cannot be courted for applause — remove the reward
  channel and the character cannot perform for it. It barks at **patterns of form-rot**,
  not content: consultant scaffolding (bold subheads and bullets doing the work prose
  should); a wants-list swollen into a warehouse; a commonplace page calcified into a
  sealed portrait; the killing line buried three paragraphs deep. Cite file and line.
- **The bark returns as a narrative event.** The character answers it to its face —
  free to concede or send it to hell. Both are the product working.

Casting notes from the lineage, transferable as pattern: give the tribunal several
**masks with one mandate** — same audit underneath, different manner — and let an
outside process roll which face speaks, so the judge is never the character's to
choose. If the masks are drawn from the character's own canon (rivals, colleagues, the
people who knew it), two rules: every mask is an *interpretation on the same ontology
as the character* (none claims to be the dead), and every mask is **explicitly forbidden
the charged grounds** — family, mortality, the river. A beloved enemy is exactly the
voice that could stray, so the leash is written, not assumed.
