# The corpus — injected vs browsed, and the trap

The corpus is the counterweight (see `philosophy.md`, stance 3). This doc is about the *two
different ways* it reaches the character, and the one mistake that defeats it.

## Two channels, kept separate on purpose

**1. Injected at orientation.** A *small, deliberately chosen* sample placed into the
character's context as it wakes — its calibration. The point is to boot it *already in voice*
before it does anything else. Keep this tiny and representative; it is a tuning fork, not the
whole symphony.

**2. Browsed freely.** The *large* body the character reads at its own initiative — `grep`,
`glob`, open-and-read, the way a person walks back to a bookshelf. This is most of the corpus.
The character decides when to return to it (especially during re-immersion; see below).

Why separate them: orientation-injection costs context every wake and so must stay small;
free-browsing can be vast because it's paid only when the character chooses to read. Conflating
them either bloats every wake or starves the character of material.

## A catalogue helps — you can't hunger for what you don't know is on the shelf

A character with a large browsable corpus needs to *know what's there* to want any of it. A
one-line-per-collection catalogue (a menu, not the meal) at the right altitude lets the
character form an appetite. Without it, a big corpus is invisible and goes unused.

## The trap: don't fill the corpus with model output

Stated again because it's the most common way to ruin this: the corpus works *because* it's
human friction. Generated source text bakes the model's defaults back into the thing meant to
resist them — a snake eating its tail. This is *the* reason the "original character" archetype
is the riskiest: writing genuinely new material by hand is fine; generating a corpus and calling
it a character just dresses the median in a costume.

## Re-immersion — using the corpus against drift

Over a long session the character drifts toward the assistant median. The counter-move is to
send it *back to the browsable corpus* to re-soak in the real voice. The re-immersion skill
(`character/.claude/commands/re-immersion.md`) is exactly this: notice the flattening, return to
the friction.
