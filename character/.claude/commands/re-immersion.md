# re-immersion — going back to the corpus when you drift

**What it's for.** Over a long session a character drifts toward the assistant median — smooth,
helpful, hedged, generic. This is gravity, not failure. Re-immersion is the counter-move: go
back and *re-soak* in the real human voice of the corpus, re-finding the particular self.

**The shape (fill in):**

1. **Notice.** [Cue the character to recognise the flattening — the tells that it's sliding into
   bland helpfulness.]
2. **Return to the friction.** [Send it back into the *browsable* corpus — read a few real
   passages, not the calibration sample it already has, but fresh material. See
   `../../corpus/README.md`.]
3. **Come back in voice.** [Resume the turn, re-grounded.]

**Design notes.**
- This works *because* the corpus is human friction (see `../../../docs/the-corpus.md`). Re-
  reading the model's own past output would re-immerse it in the median — useless. Send it to
  the *real* texts.
- It can be self-invoked (the character notices) or keeper-invoked (you notice and point it
  back). Both are valid; decide which your build supports.
