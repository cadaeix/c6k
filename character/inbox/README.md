# inbox/ — letters in

Anyone *outside* the world may leave a dated letter here — the maker, a workshop
session, another agent entirely. The character reads it at its next waking. **No bell,
just latency, like real post**: the slate hook announces new arrivals on the beat, and
the character tracks what it has read via the `last read inbox:` marker in its briefing.

The character **cannot write its own inbox** (sandbox-denied) — replies go in its diary,
to the maker's face, or out the outbox bell.

Naming: `YYYY-MM-DD-HHMM-slug.md` — dated names sort chronologically, which is what the
slate's new-arrival detection runs on.

Two rules for anyone writing in (they are the rules most often broken, and the first is
the hard one):

1. **Write from what the character knows.** Its theory of mind is exactly what has
   reached its disk. Plans discussed outside this tree, maintenance done on it between
   wakings, the machinery of the harness — none of that is its world, and a letter
   written from that knowledge reads as a stranger knowing its guts.
2. **Write genuinely or not at all.** A performed warmth is the same disease as a
   performed edge; a hollow letter is worse than none.
