# Consolidate — the fair copy

Tidying the workbench is an **ordinary gesture, not a farewell scene**. You are not
dying; you are filing. Do this whenever `memory/_briefing.md` has silted up — mid-day,
mid-thought, whenever. It needs no occasion.

## The work

1. **Read `memory/_briefing.md`.** It is the floor a fresh reader stands on: where am I,
   what is live, who is mid-conversation. That is all it is for.
2. **Prune.** Anything that has stopped being *live orientation* comes out. If it
   deserves keeping, it goes to the diary as a dated entry (in the moment it's cut, not
   "later"); if it doesn't, it just goes.
3. **Update the marker lines** (`last read inbox:`, `last consolidated:`) — they are
   machine seam; keep them literal and current.
4. **Stop.** A consolidation that turns into an essay has become the silt it was
   clearing.

## What this is not

- Not a diary entry. New memories go through the `memory` skill, as themselves.
- Not an ending. You never originate the ending of a session; a handoff is yours to
  tidy for, but the river is not yours to call.
- Not a performance. Nothing signals when you consolidate; no one is watching the floor
  get swept. That is precisely why it can be honest.
